The United Kingdom

The United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland is the official name of the country situated in the British Isles. The two main islands are Great Britain and Ireland. They are separated from the continent by the English Channel and the Strait of Dover. The UK consists of 4 parts - England, Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland.
In the north the country is washed by the Atlantic Ocean, in the west - by the Irish Sea, in the east - by the North Sea. When you travel about the country you can see different kinds of scenery: highlands and lowlands, forests and hills, meadows and plains. Britain is an unusually beautiful country! There are no very long rivers in the UK. The most important rivers are the Thames (the deepest) and the Severn (the longest). The capital of England - London - stands on the Thames. Each part of the UK has its own capital. The capital of Scotland is Edinburgh, the capital of Wales is Cardiff and the capital of Northern Ireland is Belfast.
The population of the UK is about 60 million people. Many of them live in big industrial cities such as Birmingham, Manchester, Liverpool, York and Newcastle. Some English towns are known all over the world though they are not very big. For example, Stratford-upon-Avon is Shakespeare's birthplace and Cambridge and Oxford are famous for their universities.
Great Britain is a parliamentary monarchy. Officially the head of state is the Queen. But the real power in the country belongs to the British Parliament and the British Government. The British Parliament consists of two Houses - the House of Lords and the House of Commons. The head of the government is the Prime Minister.
The national flag of the United Kingdom is the Union Jack. It is made up of three crosses: the cross of St. George (the patron saint of England), the cross of St.
Andrew (the patron saint of Scotland and the cross of St. Patrick (the patron saint of Ireland).
